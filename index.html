About

I’m Eldrex Delos Reyes Bula, a creative thinker at the crossroads of technology, design, and innovation. My journey began with a deep curiosity about the world—exploring philosophy and poetry, which shaped the way I think, question, and create. Over time, I’ve brought that mindset into the realm of technology, combining critical thinking with hands-on creativity to build digital solutions that are not only functional but also intuitive and meaningful.

I’m passionate about designing and developing tools that make life easier—whether it’s through seamless user experiences, intelligent automation, or innovative software. Every project I take on is driven by a purpose: to solve real problems, simplify the complex, and bring ideas to life in ways that truly matter. I don’t just build for the sake of it; I create with intention—ensuring that technology always serves people in the best possible way.

Core Philosophies

  • Purpose-Driven Design: Every pixel and line of code must serve a meaningful human need
  • Holistic Thinking: Bridging technical precision with artistic sensibility creates truly innovative solutions
  • Iterative Growth: Continuous learning and adaptation are non-negotiable in our evolving digital landscape

My Poems

A Glimpse of You

I tried to hide this indefinable affection,

But you genuinely captured my naive attention,

You were an angel resembles near perfection,

Undeniably you’re an epitome of a rare inspiration.

It was your shimmering eyes that stunned me for a while,

You always captivating me with your vivid smile,

The charm of you is running me out of the rhyme,

Besides, your vigorous heart owe these feelings to bind.

I wish you knew the way I felt,

Whenever my sight touches you, something’s different,

You pinched my heart with that perfect moment,

A glimpse that my mind will never forget.

And if the universe force our way to meet,

When my heart producing that same beat,

Stretching down into the abyss, I won’t forfeit,

For the love that will make my world complete.

Two in One, Love as One

A mother so strong, she could stand as a father,

A father so kind, like a mother, he'd gather.

Two hands, two hearts, yet one steady ground,

In their love, my world is found.

My nanay, a warrior, fierce and bright,

Holding the stars in the darkest night.

With arms like steel, yet soft as air,

She lifts me up with endless care.

My tatay, the shelter when storms arise,

With quiet strength and knowing eyes.

A voice so firm yet warm as the sun,

Teaching me battles are fought, but never run.

Side by side, they carve the way,

Through trials, through laughter, come what may.

One love, one fight, no task too grand,

With them, I walk—proud where I stand.

For in my heart, their light will stay,

Guiding me through every day.

Two in one, yet love as one,

Forever shining, never undone.

UNBECOMING

You wear your cap like a crown of thorns,

tassel dangling—a noose of almosts.

Your diploma, a map of roads you’ll never walk,

inked in the dust of what if and not yet.

Breathe in the auditorium’s stale applause—

how it smells of erasers and last chances.

Your best friend’s laugh, trapped in Polaroid glue,

while your mouth stitches “We’ll keep in touch”

into a lie smooth as a fresh-linked chain.

You were taught to bloom, but no one mentioned

how roots scream when ripped from familiar dirt.

You packed your childhood in a dorm-bound box,

labeled Fragile in your mother’s trembling hand.

(Do you even know what you’ve buried?)

The future is a hallway of locked doors.

You knock, but your fists are still soft

from clutching textbooks like prayer books.

You whisper, “I’m ready,” but your voice cracks

like the spine of a journal you never finished.

At night, you dissect your reflection:

'Who is this stranger wearing my skin?'

Her eyes are borrowed, his laughter rehearsed—

a pantomime of the person you swore you’d become.

(You miss the 'you' that loved without calculus.)

Graduation is a funeral where you’re both corpse and mourner.

You bury versions of yourself in every handshake:

the artist who traded brushes for spreadsheets,

the rebel who now quotes corporate manifestos,

the lover who unlearned their own heartbeat.

Here’s the truth they’ll never frame in gold:

Growing up is just folding yourself smaller

until you fit in the world’s clenched fist.

You’ll chase “success” like a sleepwalker chasing dawn,

numb to the ache of roads untaken.

So cry. Cry hard. Let saltwater baptize

the ghost of the 'you' you abandoned at 18.

For adulthood is a requiem in reverse—

you’ll spend decades mourning the person

who stares back, raw and unrecognizable,

from the rearview mirror’s merciless glass.

Published: February 15, 2025

Tony the Sleepy Tiger

Deep in the jungle, late at night,

Tony the Tiger was feeling just right.

Scrolling his phone with a mindless grin,

Watching cat videos (was that his kin?).


The Owl hooted, “Go to bed, my friend,

This nonsense binge must come to an end!”

But Tony just yawned, “Oh, one more meme...

And maybe two... or seventeen...”

By 3 AM, his eyes were fried,

His brain was mush, but still, he tried—

“To sleep? Pfft, nah, I’ll be just fine!”

Then promptly dozed off at 3:09.


At 7 AM, the jungle roared,

But Tony? He snored and snored!

The Wolf howled, “Rise and shine, big guy!”

Tony just groaned, “Let me die…”

The Fox smirked, “Lazy cat, wake up!”

“NO,” grumbled Tony, hugging his cup.

“I need coffee, or I swear I’ll scream—

Or just stay here and sleep... and dream...”

The Eagle swooped with a mighty screech,

And Snake hissed, “Get up, you leech!”

But Tony just lay there, dead to the earth,

Wondering, Was life even worth... waking up for?

The jungle gave up and let him be,

Till noon arrived—and so did he.

Stretching and yawning, he mumbled, “I’m up...

Now where’s my breakfast? And my nap cup?”
